My Story

Hey, you've found me!

It's nice to meet you!
My name is Bonnie Peng and I am an aspiring medical software developer, a perfect blend of both of my passions in healthcare and tech.
I aim to improve global health through code and ensure all new medical technologies are more effective than the last.

Currently living in Halifax, Nova Scotia, I love spending my free time with my camera at the waterfront, kidnapping my friends' dogs for hikes, gazing at the beauty of dark mode IDE's and telling people I like coding in dark mode better than light mode so I don't lose my friends ;)

My Passions

photography

Photography very quickly became one of the biggest parts of my life, and it's a hobby that I know I'll never let go of. The liberty, the inspiring community, and the thrill of its possibilities continuously draws me to my camera and into a new world with every picture.

Portrait shoots are easily my favourite, and I'm proudly running a business which provides free photoshoots for aspiring actors and models to help kickstart their portfolio.

design & coding

My passion for creating something of my own drew me right to programming!
Integrating personality and creativity into technology is one of my biggest goals as a programmer, and I hope to use this side of tech to inspire those around me, and break the stereotype that tech is a scary industry.

I'm currently pursuing a B.Sc. in Computer Science at the University of Waterloo, which I hope to extend into a masters degree.

medicine

I've always been curious about the world of medicine (I originally planned go to med school!) and dreamed of working alongside the medical professionals who have supported me all my life.

I always strive to engage in healthcare communities, and I'm very happy to be the Web Designer for uWaterloo's Women in Healthcare club! Joining this community has allowed me to learn so much about my peers in medicine, as well as participate in panel talks with female healthcare leaders across the country.

I'll tell you a secret - I've always thought that the intersection of health and technology would be my particular place in the world, and I can't wait to see where this path takes me.

Android Studio


Alright let me just get this out there... Android Studio's drag-and-drop framework is EVERYTHING I'VE EVER NEEDED. It's so useful to see how your app will look as you're coding it, and for the most part you don't have to code much at all! As much as I like front-end, this feature is the biggest time-saver and gives such a nice mental break when you can kick back with some music and just piece together your app like a puzzle.
